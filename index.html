I’m a PhD student in Machine Learning at the University of Edinburgh, School of Informatics. My supervisors are Dr. Charles Sutton and Prof. Chris Williams. Current research focus on Deep Generative Modelling and Variational Inference under Noise, more generally Robust Machine Learning.

I have a BSc. and MSc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Instituto Superior Tecnico (IST), Portugal. My MSc thesis was on wireless networks, exploring resource management of the radio spectrum: constrained convex optimization, integer programming, queuing theory. I also have a MSc(R) in Data Science from University of Edinburgh, work on probabilistic modelling for integrity constraints (outlier detection).

I was a Signal Processing Software Engineer, GNSS receivers, at GMV (Lisbon). Mostly worked for a European Space Agency research project: time-series data, estimation models (statistical and DSP). I also was a Research Assistant (IST) in convex optimisation applied to image processing, using proximal algorithms.
Interests. Machine Learning: Deep Generative Models, Bayesian Inference, Robust ML, Anomaly Detection, Causality, Unsupervised Learning, Meta Learning, Relational Learning, Weak-Supervision; Optimization. Applications: Signal Processing, Healthcare, Networks.
